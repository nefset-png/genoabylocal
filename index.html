The Riviera, the way it should be seen

We leave Genoa by ferry at 10:00 from Porto Antico and spend the day exploring Portofino, the hidden cove of San Fruttuoso, and the charming fishing village of Camogli — returning by the last ferry at 17:30. No tour bus, no fixed script. Just us, moving at our own pace.

Portofino is popular and in high season you'll feel it. But there's a real difference between arriving with a group on a schedule and arriving with someone who knows where to go, when to go, and which corners most visitors never reach.

Ferry — included

Golfo Paradiso ferry from Porto Antico. Scenic crossing along the Riviera.

Portofino

The iconic village seen with local eyes — the harbor, the viewpoints, the corners most tourists never reach.

San Fruttuoso di Camogli

A medieval abbey in a hidden cove, reachable only by boat — extraordinary.

Camogli

A real Ligurian fishing village — colourful houses, pebble beach and focaccia worth travelling for.

FAQ

Good to know

What time does it start and end?
We meet at Porto Antico at 09:30. Ferry departs at 10:00. Last ferry back from Camogli is at 17:30, arriving in Genoa around 18:30.
Are ferry tickets included?
Yes — all ferry tickets for the day are included in your price. The ferry is a public tourist boat (Golfo Paradiso) shared with other passengers, but we move independently.
Will Portofino be crowded?
In high season, yes. I offer timing, local knowledge and routes that most visitors never take — it makes a real difference.
Is food included?
Food and drinks are not included unless agreed in advance. A full lunch at a local restaurant in Camogli can be added as an optional extra — just mention it when booking.
What if the weather is bad?
In rough weather, Golfo Paradiso may cancel or modify the service. I monitor conditions and will let you know in advance. Rescheduling with no penalty.

Three villages. One day. Your pace.

We take the train from Genoa and spend the day in Cinque Terre — exploring Vernazza, Manarola and Riomaggiore at a relaxed, unhurried pace. No tour group schedule, no headset, no rushing. Just the villages as they actually are.

Cinque Terre is famous, and in high season the crowds are real. What I offer is timing, local knowledge and the ability to adapt the day to what you actually want.

Train from Genoa

Direct regional train from Genova Brignole — about 1.5 hours, coastal views.

Vernazza, Manarola, Riomaggiore

Three villages, each with its own character. Explored properly, not rushed.

Hiking option

Walk coastal trail sections between villages — adapted to your fitness on the day.

Local food stops

Anchovies, pesto, local wine — I'll point you to the right spots throughout the day.

How is the price structured?
The price is per group, not per person — from €600 for up to 4 people. Train tickets add approx. €60–70 per person on top if you choose the full logistics option.
Are train tickets included?
It depends on the option. The base price does not include train tickets. The full logistics option includes all tickets, prepared in advance.
Will it be crowded?
In high season, yes — crowds are part of the reality. I know the timing, the less-visited paths and the quieter corners that make a real difference.
Is food included?
Food and drinks are not included unless agreed in advance. I'll guide you to curated local spots throughout the day.
Can we add Monterosso and dinner?
Yes — in summer (June–September) we can end in Monterosso with a swim and dinner. Extra cost, arranged in advance. Mention it when booking.
